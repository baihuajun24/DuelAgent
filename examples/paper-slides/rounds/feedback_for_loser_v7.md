Feedback for Version B (HTML)

- Raise the on-slide typography to the rubric floor throughout the deck. Several places still rely on 14-18px text, including figure captions, process labels, setup cards, appendix text, and support copy on the title slide; increase these to room-readable sizes and move secondary detail into notes.
- Enlarge and simplify the `Background: The Inference Cycle with Tools` diagram. The stage labels and the one-line footer are too small for back-of-room viewing, so use fewer words per box and give the flow more vertical space.
- Trim `Experimental Setup` aggressively. Keep only the variables the audience needs to interpret the plots, and move hardware specs, GPU allocation, and sweep details into speaker notes or appendix.
- Split `Strengths & Limitations` into two separate slides, or cut each side to the two most important points. The current slide carries two ideas at once and forces the body text smaller than the design target.
- Rewrite the `Discussion Questions` cards into shorter prompts. Each card should be a discussion starter, not a paragraph; reduce each to one crisp question plus a short qualifier.
- Reduce the amount of text on `Summary`. Five full bullets makes the slide read-heavy at the end of the talk; compress this into a headline plus three larger takeaways.
- Rework the backup slides for live readability. The proof sketch, assumptions, per-optimization savings, and API fields are all useful, but the current appendix text is too dense and too small to use comfortably during Q&A.
- Audit every figure for embedded legend and axis readability. When the original paper figure has small internal labels, crop tighter or add a local annotation so the trend is legible without zooming.
- Increase the figure caption size. The takeaway wording is strong, but captions at around 14px are undersized for projected presentation use.
- Reclaim space from low-value chrome. The repeated bottom roadmap tabs and small footer elements consume slide area that would be better spent on larger figures, larger captions, or more whitespace.
- Bring the main-deck pacing closer to the 16-20 content-slide target for a 25-minute talk. Right now several combined slides are carrying too much material; adding one or two split slides would improve pacing and readability.
